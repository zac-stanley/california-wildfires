Wildfires in California

Greater than 10,000 Acres 1878-2018

This map is meant to show the wide distribution and size of large of wildfires in California over the last 140 years. It is difficult to show the specific extent of each fire and their size at statewide scale. To ameliorate this, graduated symbols were used to give a sense of fire size in acres ranging from 10,000 to 500,000. When zoomed in the map dynamically changes to polygons that are labeled with fire name, size in acres and year of occurence. The muted basemap is meant provide geographic context while keeping the focus on the bright orange fire features.

The methodology for the map was quite simple. A geodatabase feature class of polygons is downloaded from the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection's Fire and Resource Assessment Program (FRAP). It is converted to shapefile and cleaned up keeping only the fields necessary to display data as described above. The polygons are converted to points at the centroid of each polygon. Both polygon and point datasets are filtered by fire size to only show fires >= 10,0000 acres.

Once data prep is complete the files are zipped and uploaded to Mapbox as tilesets. Dynamic scaling across zoom range is applied to points, polygons, and custom labels to show appopriate level of detail based on scale.
